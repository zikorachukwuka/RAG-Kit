# NHF (National Housing Fund) — Nigeria

## What is NHF?
The National Housing Fund (NHF) is a scheme administered by the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN). It allows workers to save towards homeownership and access mortgage loans at low interest rates.

## Is NHF mandatory?
NHF is OPTIONAL for private sector employees under the Business Facilitation Act 2022. Private sector employers must not tell employees that NHF is compulsory. Employees choose whether to opt in.

NHF is mandatory only for public sector employees (federal and state civil servants).

## Contribution rate
If an employee opts in: 2.5% of their basic salary per month, deducted by the employer and remitted to FMBN.

## Due date
NHF contributions must be remitted to FMBN within one month of deduction.

## How to register and remit
Employees who opt in must first register with FMBN to obtain an NHF registration number. The employer then deducts 2.5% of the basic salary and remits it to FMBN using the employee's registration number.

## Benefits for employees who opt in
- Access to low-interest mortgage loans through FMBN (currently 6% per annum)
- Refund of NHF contributions plus 2% interest if the employee retires without taking a mortgage
- Participation in the NHF estate housing scheme

## Common mistakes
- Telling employees NHF is mandatory — it is optional for private sector workers
- Deducting NHF from employees who have not opted in
- Calculating NHF on gross salary instead of basic salary

## Summary
Only deduct and remit NHF for employees who have voluntarily chosen to participate. Document their opt-in decision. Never impose NHF deductions on private sector employees.
