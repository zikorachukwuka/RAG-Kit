# WHT (Withholding Tax) on Contractor Payments — Nigeria

## What is WHT?
Withholding Tax (WHT) is a tax deducted at source from payments made to contractors and service providers. It is an advance payment of the contractor's income tax liability, remitted to the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) by the paying company.

## WHT rate for contractors
5% of the gross payment made to the contractor.

## Who deducts WHT?
The paying company (the business making the payment) deducts WHT from the contractor's invoice and remits it to FIRS. The contractor receives the net amount after WHT deduction.

## Example
A contractor invoices ₦500,000 for services rendered.
- WHT deducted: 5% x ₦500,000 = ₦25,000
- Amount paid to contractor: ₦500,000 - ₦25,000 = ₦475,000
- Amount remitted to FIRS: ₦25,000

## Due date
WHT deducted in a given month must be remitted to FIRS by the 21st of the following month. January WHT is due by 21 February.

## How to remit WHT
WHT is remitted to FIRS via TaxPro Max, the FIRS online tax platform (taxpromax.firs.gov.ng). The company must generate a WHT credit note for each contractor after remittance.

## WHT credit notes
After remitting WHT to FIRS, the paying company issues a WHT credit note to the contractor. The contractor uses this credit note to offset their corporate or personal income tax liability when filing annual returns with FIRS.

## Does WHT apply to all contractors?
WHT at 5% applies to payments for services — consulting, professional services, technical services, management fees, construction, and similar engagements. It does not apply to purchases of goods.

## Contractor vs employee distinction
Contractors (independent service providers) are subject to WHT, not PAYE. They are not on the payroll, do not receive pension contributions, and are not included in NSITF calculations. If a contractor works exclusively for a business over a long period and is indistinguishable from an employee, FIRS may reclassify the relationship and require PAYE treatment.

## Common mistakes
- Forgetting to deduct WHT before paying the contractor
- Missing the 21st-of-the-month remittance deadline
- Failing to issue WHT credit notes to contractors
- Deducting WHT on goods purchases (WHT applies to services, not goods)
