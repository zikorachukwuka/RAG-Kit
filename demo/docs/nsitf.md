# NSITF (Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund) — Nigeria

## What is NSITF?
The Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund (NSITF) provides compensation to employees who suffer workplace injuries, disabilities, or death arising from employment. It is governed by the NSITF Act and managed by the NSITF Board.

## Who pays NSITF?
NSITF is an employer-only obligation. Employees do not contribute. The employer pays 1% of the total monthly payroll.

## Contribution rate
1% of total monthly payroll, paid entirely by the employer.

Total monthly payroll means the sum of all gross salaries paid to all employees in that month.

## Due date
NSITF contributions for a given month are due on the 1st of the following month. January contributions are due by 1 February.

## How to remit NSITF
Employers remit NSITF contributions to the NSITF Board through designated bank channels or the NSITF online portal. The employer must be registered with NSITF to make remittances.

## Employee registration
All employees must be registered with NSITF. Registration is done by the employer on behalf of the employee. Registered employees receive an NSITF employee number.

## Benefits for employees
Registered employees are entitled to:
- Compensation for temporary total disability from workplace injury
- Compensation for permanent partial or total disability
- Death benefits paid to dependants of employees who die from work-related causes
- Medical expenses arising from workplace accidents

## Penalty for non-compliance
Failure to register or remit attracts penalties including fines and prosecution under the NSITF Act.

## Common questions

**Does NSITF apply to casual workers?**
Yes. NSITF applies to all employees, including casual workers, as long as they are on the payroll.

**Does NSITF apply to contractors?**
Contractors are not employees and are typically not included in the NSITF payroll calculation. However, if a contractor works exclusively for your business over a long period, they may be reclassified as employees.
