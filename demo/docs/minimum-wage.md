# Minimum Wage — Nigeria

## Current minimum wage
The national minimum wage in Nigeria is **₦70,000 per month**, effective from the Minimum Wage Act 2024 signed into law by President Bola Tinubu.

## Who does it apply to?
The minimum wage applies to all employees in both the public and private sectors. Every worker on the payroll must earn at least ₦70,000 gross per month.

## Does it apply to part-time workers?
The minimum wage is set on a monthly basis. For part-time workers, the proportionate hourly rate should be consistent with the minimum wage standard. There is no separate statutory minimum for part-time hourly rates, but employers should ensure part-time total pay is fair and defensible.

## Does it apply to casual workers?
Yes. Casual workers on the payroll are entitled to pay consistent with the minimum wage. A casual worker engaged for a full month should not earn less than ₦70,000.

## Does it apply to contractors?
Contractors are not employees and the minimum wage law does not directly apply to contractor payments. Contractor fees are negotiated and governed by the contract agreement.

## What happens if a worker earns below the minimum wage?
Paying below the minimum wage is a violation of the Minimum Wage Act. Employees can report non-compliant employers to the Federal Ministry of Labour and Employment. Employers may face fines, prosecution, or back-pay orders.

## Common questions

**Can an employee agree to work below minimum wage?**
No. A contract clause setting pay below the minimum wage is void and unenforceable.

**Does the minimum wage include allowances?**
The ₦70,000 refers to total monthly gross pay, not just basic salary. Allowances can form part of the total as long as gross pay reaches the minimum threshold.

**When was ₦70,000 set?**
The ₦70,000 minimum wage was established by the Minimum Wage Act 2024, replacing the previous ₦30,000 rate.
