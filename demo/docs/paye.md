# PAYE (Pay As You Earn) — Nigeria

## What is PAYE?
PAYE is the income tax deducted from employee salaries by employers on behalf of the relevant State Internal Revenue Service (SIRS). It is governed by the Personal Income Tax Act (PITA) as amended, and the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 (NTA 2025), effective 1 January 2026.

## Who pays PAYE?
All employees earning income from employment in Nigeria are subject to PAYE — full-time, part-time, fixed-term, and casual workers. Contractors are excluded from PAYE and are subject to Withholding Tax (WHT) instead.

## PAYE Rates (NTA 2025 — effective 1 January 2026)

Annual chargeable income up to ₦800,000 is fully exempt (0%).

Progressive bands above the exemption threshold:

| Annual Chargeable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| First ₦4,000,000 | 15% |
| Next ₦4,000,000 | 18% |
| Next ₦12,000,000 | 20% |
| Next ₦30,000,000 | 22% |
| Above ₦50,000,000 | 25% |

## How is chargeable income calculated?
Chargeable income = Gross salary minus allowable deductions:
- Employee pension contribution (8% of monthly emoluments)
- NHF contribution (2.5% of basic salary — only if the employee has opted in)
- Rent relief (20% of annual rent paid, capped at ₦500,000 per year — employee must provide a lease agreement and payment receipts)

## PAYE Due Date
PAYE deducted in a given month must be remitted to the relevant State IRS by the 10th of the following month. January PAYE is due by 10 February, and so on.

## Which State IRS receives the payment?
PAYE is remitted to the State IRS of the state where the employee resides, not where the employer is located. Employees in different states require separate remittances to each state.

## Employer obligations
- Deduct PAYE from employee salaries every month
- Remit to the correct State IRS by the 10th of the following month
- File annual returns with the relevant State IRS
- Issue tax deduction receipts to employees

## Common mistakes
- Remitting to the wrong state — must be the employee's state of residence
- Missing the 10th-of-the-month deadline, which attracts penalties and interest
- Failing to apply the rent relief deduction when an employee qualifies
- Treating NHF as mandatory — it is optional for private sector employees under the Business Facilitation Act 2022
