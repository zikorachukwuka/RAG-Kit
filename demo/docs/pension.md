# Pension Contributions — Nigeria

## What is the pension scheme?
Nigeria operates a Contributory Pension Scheme (CPS) governed by the Pension Reform Act 2014. Both employers and employees contribute a percentage of the employee's monthly emoluments into a Retirement Savings Account (RSA) managed by a Pension Fund Administrator (PFA) chosen by the employee.

## Contribution rates
- Employee contribution: 8% of monthly emoluments
- Employer contribution: minimum 10% of monthly emoluments

Monthly emoluments include basic salary, housing allowance, and transport allowance.

## Who must contribute to pension?
Pension applies to:
- Full-time employees
- Part-time employees
- Fixed-term employees
- Casual workers engaged for 30 or more days

Pension does not apply to:
- Contractors (independent service providers)
- Casual workers genuinely engaged for fewer than 30 days

## Employer threshold
Pension is mandatory for employers with 15 or more workers. For employers with 3 to 15 workers, the law is silent — pension is not explicitly required but is strongly advisable. For employers with fewer than 3 workers, pension contributions are voluntary.

As a best practice, many employers calculate and remit pension for all qualifying workers regardless of headcount.

## Due date
Pension contributions must be remitted within 7 days of salary payment. If salaries are paid on the 25th, pension must be remitted by 1st of the following month.

## How to remit pension
Each employee chooses their own PFA. The employer remits the combined employee and employer contributions directly to that PFA using the employee's RSA PIN. If employees use different PFAs, separate remittances are required for each PFA.

## Penalty for late remittance
Late pension remittances attract a penalty of 2% of the total contribution per month of delay, payable to the National Pension Commission (PenCom).

## Key point on casual workers
A casual worker must be marked as pension-exempt only if they are genuinely engaged for fewer than 30 days. If the engagement extends to 30 or more days — even if originally intended to be short — pension contributions become mandatory.
